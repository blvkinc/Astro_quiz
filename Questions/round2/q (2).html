Question 2:

A galaxy is observed to have a redshift of z = 0.5. Calculate the recession velocity of the galaxy in km/s.

Answer: The redshift (z) of a galaxy is related to its recession velocity (v) through Hubble's law:

v = H0d,

where H0 is the Hubble constant and d is the distance to the galaxy.

The relation between redshift and distance is given by:

z = Δλ/λ,

where Δλ is the observed shift in wavelength and λ is the rest wavelength.

By rearranging the equation, we can express the recession velocity in terms of the redshift:

v = c × z,

where c is the speed of light.

Substituting the given redshift value (z = 0.5) and the speed of light, we can calculate the recession velocity of the galaxy in km/s.
