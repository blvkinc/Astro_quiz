Question 1:

Calculate the total available nuclear energy of the Sun

To calculate the total available nuclear energy of the Sun, we need to consider the mass deficit resulting from the fusion of hydrogen into helium and the percentage of hydrogen undergoing fusion reactions.

The mass deficit is given as 0.7% of the mass of the constituent hydrogen atoms. This means that for every 100 grams of hydrogen that undergo fusion, 0.7 grams are converted into energy according to Einstein's famous equation, E = mc^2.

We are also given that 10% of the hydrogen in the Sun undergoes fusion reactions. So, we can calculate the total available nuclear energy as follows:

1. Determine the mass of the Sun's hydrogen: Let's assume the mass of the Sun is approximately 2 x 10^30 kilograms, and the Sun is composed of about 70% hydrogen. Therefore, the mass of hydrogen in the Sun is: Mass of hydrogen = 0.7 x (2 x 10^30 kg) = 1.4 x 10^30 kg

2. Calculate the mass of hydrogen undergoing fusion reactions: Mass of hydrogen undergoing fusion = 0.1 x (1.4 x 10^30 kg) = 1.4 x 10^29 kg

3. Determine the energy released per unit mass: Energy released per unit mass (E/m) =

(0.7% mass deficit) x (c^2)

= 0.007 x (3 x 10^8 m/s)^2

= 0.007 x (9 x 10^16 m^2/s^2)

= 6.3 x 10^14 J/kg

4. Calculate the total available nuclear energy:

Total available nuclear energy = (Energy released per unit mass) x (Mass of hydrogen undergoing fusion)

= (6.3 x 10^14 J/kg) x (1.4 x 10^29 kg)

= 8.82 x 10^43 J

Therefore, the total available nuclear energy of the Sun is approximately 8.82 x 10^43 Joules.
