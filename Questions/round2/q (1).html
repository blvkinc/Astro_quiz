Question 1:

Consider a star with a mass of 2 solar masses and a radius of 10 solar radii. Assuming the star is a perfect blackbody, calculate the surface temperature of the star.

Answer: To calculate the surface temperature of the star, we can use the Stefan-Boltzmann law, which relates the luminosity of a blackbody to its surface temperature. The Stefan-Boltzmann law is given by:

L = 4πR^2σT^4,

where L is the luminosity, R is the radius, σ is the Stefan-Boltzmann constant, and T is the surface temperature.

We know that the luminosity of the star is given by:

L = 4πR^2σT^4.

Substituting the given values of mass (M) and radius (R) of the star, we can use the mass-luminosity relation for main sequence stars:

L = L☉(M/M☉)^3.5,

where L☉ is the luminosity of the Sun and M☉ is the mass of the Sun.

By substituting the values and solving the equations, we can find the surface temperature of the star.
